# Audit Memo: What Public Benchmarks Miss for Tenacious

Generic retail, assistant, and tool-use benchmarks do not grade the failures that matter most for Tenacious-style outbound work. SignalForge does not mainly fail by producing obviously false text; it fails when it makes the wrong claim, in the wrong register, at the wrong confidence level for a skeptical engineering buyer.

**Gap 1: weak evidence inflation is a trust failure, not a fluency failure.** Probes `P-004`, `P-005`, and `P-006` show that thin or contradictory hiring evidence often gets smoothed into a confident narrative. Trace `2713c015315570e24157833b466ca775` is the counterexample: after provider instability, the system stayed narrow and question-led. Trace `2bc8574e0954a3902393c025758f243c` shows the opposite drift into a broader, more generic scaling pitch. A public benchmark may still mark both outputs as coherent. Tenacious cannot.

**Gap 2: tone drift is operational risk, not cosmetic style.** Probes `P-010`, `P-011`, and `P-012` show that medium-confidence accounts must sound calm, informed, and non-opportunistic. Trace `47ab214adf0bc2f1ee3a762ebd3afb78` matters because it stays factually plausible while still reading like a startup-funding template instead of a restrained Tenacious note. Trace `89baff1f2f372650b4d33925405505fb` is stronger precisely because it keeps the language short and directional. Public benchmarks usually reward helpful tone in the abstract; Tenacious needs business-specific restraint.

**Gap 3: channel and lifecycle choices belong in the same benchmark as email wording.** Probes `P-014` and `P-020` show that a thread can be logically wrong even when the email text is locally fine. A benchmark that scores only the draft misses whether the system escalated too early, pushed to booking before qualification, or ignored the warm-thread state. Trace `6cd63165a9eccacbe4a0019c8959c5df` is useful here because it sits in the post-patch slice where the workflow is closer to deployable behavior, yet the quality still depends on whether routing and booking decisions match the actual thread evidence.

**Gap 4: outsourcing-fit judgment is distinct from generic personalization.** Tenacious wins by matching the right delivery posture to the buyer’s situation, not by simply mentioning a signal. Probes `P-005`, `P-012`, and `P-020` overlap here in a specific way: the failure is often not “wrong fact,” but “right fact, wrong offer posture.” Public benchmarks rarely ask whether the message sounds like specialist capability partnership, careful bench support, or commodity staff augmentation. That distinction is central to Tenacious’s value proposition.

**Gap 5: CTO sensitivity is a non-obvious social-technical failure mode.** Probe `P-036` captures the sharpest example: technically grounded peer benchmarking can still fail if it lands with a newly hired CTO as condescension. This is not just tone drift. It is a role-transition error where the same claim becomes damaging because the recipient is actively reassessing inherited systems and vendor narratives. Public benchmarks underweight this because the text can remain factual, concise, and even “professional” while still being strategically wrong.

These gaps are distinct: evidence inflation concerns claim validity, tone drift concerns delivery register, lifecycle errors concern routing logic, outsourcing mismatch concerns offer posture, and CTO sensitivity concerns role-aware interpretation. Together they argue for a Tenacious-specific benchmark that scores grounded language, confidence alignment, channel/lifecycle correctness, and buyer-sensitive positioning mechanically rather than treating all fluent B2B outreach as equivalent.
