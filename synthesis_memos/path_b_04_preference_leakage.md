# Synthesis Memo: Preference Leakage

## What I took from the paper

This paper is the sharpest operational warning in the Path B stack. If related models generate synthetic preference data and then also judge it, the judge can become biased toward its own family’s style. That matters a lot for Tenacious-Bench because the benchmark is small enough that subtle grading bias could look like “improvement” very quickly.

Source: https://huggingface.co/papers/2502.01534

## Where I disagree

I do not disagree with the paper’s warning. My disagreement is with the common practical response, which is often too soft. In a small benchmark project, it is tempting to say “we’ll just try to vary prompts.” I do not think that is enough here. Generator family, cheap judge family, and eval-tier judge family should be deliberately separated in the methodology, even if that slows iteration.

## How it changes my design

This paper directly affects the training-data plan:

- chosen corrections should not be generated by the same family used for final evaluation,
- cheap judge filtering should be treated as triage, not final truth,
- eval-tier scoring should be saved for sealed-slice passes and spot checks.

For this repo, that means model-family rotation is not a nice-to-have note in methodology. It is part of benchmark integrity. If we ignore it, we risk training a critic that learns the taste of its synthetic teacher rather than the real Tenacious failure surface.
